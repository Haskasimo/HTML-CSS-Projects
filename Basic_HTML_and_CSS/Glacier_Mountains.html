Highest Mountains in Glacier National Park

 Mount Cleavland

Measuring at 10,466 ft (3,190 m)tall, Mount Cleveland is the highest mountain located in Glacier National Park.
It is located 5 miles to the south of the US-Canada border.
The lowest temperature is -2 degrees F in January.
The Highest temperature at 48 degrees F in July.

 Mount Stimson

The second highest peak in Glacier National Park standing at 10,147 ft (3,093 m) tall.
It is located around the 5 miles west of the continental divide.
The coldest temperature on the mountain is -14 degrees on average in the months of December and Jauary.
The hottest temperaturs are in July at around 50 degrees F.

Climbing Route

The three main routes os Stimpson are

  • Southeast Ridge Route: It begins at Beaver Woman Lake, 24.8 miles (40 km) up the Nyack Creek trail. The trail begins along Highway 2 which is near Walton Ranger Station.
  • Two Medicine Lake Route: This is a more picturesque route that starts on the eastern side of the park. The challenging aspect of this route is climbing 3000 feet (914 m)
    over the continental divide, however, the plus point of this ascent is that we get to see stunning and majestic scenery the remote area of the park has to offer.
  • West Ridge Route: It requires a 12-mile (19 km) hike from Nyack Valley.

 Kintla Peak

The Third tallest mountain in Glacier National Park, Kintla Peak stands at an impressive 10,106 ft (3,080m) high.
It is found in the remote south east corner of the park.
It's temperature is almost identical to Mount Stimson all year around.
